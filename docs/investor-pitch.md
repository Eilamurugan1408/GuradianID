## The hook (30 seconds)

You know that moment? You're in a new city. Your phone dies. Or you take a wrong turn. Or you just want your family to know you landed safe. **There was no simple way to do that.** Not without sharing your live location forever, or texting five people, or hoping they remember to check in.

**GuardianID is that one thing:** know where you are when it matters. One QR. One check-in. One SOS if things go wrong. For solo travelers, groups, and the people who love them.

---

## Why "simple" is the point

I Love PDF didn't do quantum computing. A developer was annoyed he couldn't merge two PDFs. He built a tiny tool. **Millions use it every day** because the problem was real and the solution was obvious once it existed.

I kept thinking: *Why is there no "I love knowing my people are safe when they travel"?* Forms? Manual check-ins? WhatsApp groups that go silent? That's not a product—that's a workaround. So I built the product. **GuardianID:** live safety layer, check-ins, SOS, and a single place for trips—for tourists, groups, and the agencies and insurers who serve them.

| What exists today | The gap | What we do |
|------------------|--------|------------|
| **WhatsApp groups, "text me when you land"** | No verified check-in, no trip context, no SOS in one place. | One trip, one check-in, one SOS. Family/authorities see status in real time. |

## What we built

- **QR-based identity + eKYC** — one profile, one ID, works offline.
- **Live safety layer** — geofencing, check-ins, SOS to authorities and contacts.
- **Group rooms** — shared map, chat, "everyone okay?" in one place.
- **Trip flow** — bookings, optional trip insurance (Allianz, World Nomads, AXA), receipts.
- **B2B2C** — agencies and insurers get verified trips and incident webhooks; we don’t replace them, we make them safer and more valuable.

**In one sentence:** We’re the safety layer that sits under travel—so "are you okay?" is one tap, not a guessing game.
